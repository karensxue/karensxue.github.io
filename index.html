About Me

My name is Karen Sunny Xue, I’m a rising Senior in high school, and I'm a passionate artistic swimmer. I was born and raised in California, whereas my parents came from China. In my spare time, I enjoy cooking and hanging out with friends and family. One thing I’ve been especially interested in recently is folding origami cranes. I heard a saying that if I fold 1000 cranes, my wish will come true. As I continue folding, I eagerly await for my wish to be fulfilled.

This year, I'm proud to join USA Artistic Swimming National Senior team. Prior to that I spent eight years with the Walnut Creek Aquanuts. I’ve represented the United States of America, competing at World Junior and youth Championship & Pan Am since year 2021. One of my favorite aspects about the sport is watching the process of a routine be so carefully created, starting from just a simple set of arm strokes, to three minutes of passion and artistry. My favorite skill is the barracuda, more specifically, the split rocket. So, given, my favorite elements are the two rocket elements. In a team environment, I am versatile in my positions. I’ve been trained as a base, flyer, and pusher, and I can adapt easily to new positions or counts. As the designated flyer. I have been regularly going to classes to further improve my flying skills and tricks. For more videos and information about my swimming, you can check the Art Swim page.

In terms of academics, I tend to gravitate towards STEM-related subjects, as they are something I find interest in. Other than school and swiming, I also frequently volunteer at Food Rescure and Recovery, community service, coaching young althletes swiming and providing math tutoring. Please check out my Volunteering and Tutoring page
